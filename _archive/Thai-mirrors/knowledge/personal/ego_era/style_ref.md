## 📌 Approved Writing Sample

### Chapter 1 — The Day the World Didn't Look Back

Rain in the big city is normal.

It's not heavy, but it's enough to dampen clothes, make the streets slippery, and make people on the street hurry a little more.

A small bookstore in a deep alley has half of its white neon light on, as if it doesn't want many people to come in.

Rin, a young man, sits behind a dark, old wooden counter. His fingers tap a fountain pen cap, while his eyes stare at a book left open an hour ago, but he's not reading it. He's just waiting for the day to pass.

"Do you have any other books? Cheaper ones?" the customer's voice says without looking at his face.

Rin hesitates for a moment, moves his hand away from the pen cap, and gets up to look at the bookshelf.

"Yes, I'll find one for you—"

"No need, I have to go now," the other person cuts in, then closes their phone screen.

The bell at the door rings, 'Krung' 'Kring', and silence returns.

As if no conversation ever happened in the real world.

Rin stands between the dim bookshelves, his hand still hovering in the air.

"Oh... okay..." The voice is so quiet it sounds like he's talking to himself.

Rin closes the shop early. He sits down on the counter, opens a black-covered ledger, and looks at it. The sales figure of zero for today and the overdue balance for last month reinforce the inescapable reality. Thoughts in his head loop around the warnings from those around him about the truths of capitalism, about "If you can't handle it, you have to adapt," or else just let the store disappear.

Rin closes the ledger and stares blankly at the bookshelf in front of him, even though no message truly enters his feelings. People passing by the shop window without a glance, as if this small bookstore had never existed here in the first place.

The rain outside picked up a bit more. He managed to lock the shop door and walk alone, as he always does.

People walk by, cars drive by, laughter from a group sheltering from the rain under a skyscraper's roof. Everything seems normal.

Except him, who is invisible.

Rin turns into a narrow alley, a shortcut back to his room. The streetlights inside are dimmer and more gloomy than before. The city's sound seems cut in half, only hearing the rain and his own breathing.

But as he looks up, a tiny little gray kitten is about to fall. Its legs tremble. Its small paws scratch, trying to hold onto a pipe that offers no grip, while the concrete floor below has a strong current that looks very severe. If the little cat falls, it will surely be sucked into the city's drainage system, draining its life away.

In just a split second, the kitten loses its balance and falls.

Rin doesn't think anymore. His neglected heart protests, cracking the silence.

He just feels that he must protect this life, so it doesn't get lost in the drain.

## 🔍 Style DNA Analysis (Approved Writing Style)

### 1. Sentence Rhythm
- **Ultra-short sentences** opening scenes — undecorated, unexplained, just placed.
- **Alternating long and short** sentences to guide reader breath and pacing.
- The shortest sentences carry the most weight.
  - *"He's just waiting... for the day to pass."* — spacing adds heavy pauses.
  - *"Except him, who is invisible."* — single line, the emotional core of the scene.

### 2. Environmental Grounding
- The environment is not just a backdrop — it is **visible emotion**.
- *"Half of its white neon light on, as if it doesn't want many people to come in"* — objects have feelings.
- Rain is not just weather; it represents pressing weight.

### 3. Physical Emotional Expression (Show, Don't Tell)
- Never say "he was sad" or "he felt lonely."
- Show through body language: *"his hand still hovering in the air,"* *"sits down heavily,"* *"stares blankly."*
- Speech is brief and self-contained: *"Oh... okay..."*

### 4. Dialogue Pacing
- Conversations are fragmented, broken, and uncoordinated — just like real life.
- No satisfying exchanges — just speaking and being dismissed.
- Result: Unspoken, profound pain.

### 5. Ego Manifestation (Correct Power Usage)
- No explanations, no flashy visual effects, no shouting out powers.
- Powers arise from **overflowing emotion**, not conscious decision.
- *"Rin doesn't think anymore. His neglected heart protests, cracking the silence"* — correct manifestation.

### 6. Scene Closings
- End with **emotional weight**, not plot events.
- *"He just feels that he must protect this life"* — pure EGO ERA style.
